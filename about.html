About AI Tools Insider

I test AI tools — writing, coding, image, video, you name it — and write honest reviews about what I find. No fluff, no sponsored BS, just real results from daily use.

Why This Site Exists

I built this site in 2026 because I got tired of reading AI tool reviews that were clearly paid for or written by someone who used the tool for 10 minutes. The AI space moves insanely fast — new models drop every week, pricing changes overnight, and features you relied on yesterday get deprecated tomorrow.

I'm a software developer who actually uses these tools every day — for coding, writing, generating images, editing video, automating workflows. This site is where I share what I've learned. If a tool is good, I'll say so. If it sucks, I'll say that too.

How I Test Tools

Nothing fancy. I just use the damn thing:

  • I sign up like a normal person. Free tier if available, paid plan if necessary. No special "reviewer" accounts.
  • I use it for real work. Not a demo. Not a tutorial. I actually integrate it into my workflow and see if it holds up.
  • I compare it directly. Same prompt, same task, same conditions. The winner is obvious pretty quickly.
  • I update when things change. If a tool gets significantly better or worse, I come back and update the review.

That's it. No 5-phase methodology, no standardized rubrics. Just honest, real-world testing.

A Few Things I Believe

  • Paid reviews are garbage. I don't accept money for positive coverage. If a company wants my review, they get whatever I honestly think — good or bad.
  • Affiliate links don't change my opinion. Some links on this site are affiliate links. If I recommend something, it's because I actually think it's good, not because of commission.
  • Every tool has flaws. I point them out. If I can't find anything bad to say about a tool, it means I haven't used it enough yet.

About Me

I'm a solo developer who's been building software for years and working with AI tools since they became actually useful. I'm not a big media company, not a marketing agency — just one guy who spends too much time testing AI tools and writing about them.

My background is in software engineering, which means I tend to be more technical in my reviews of coding and developer tools. But I also write about AI writing assistants, image generators, video tools, and productivity apps — basically anything that saves me time or makes my work better.

I update this site whenever I have something useful to say. Sometimes that's multiple times a week, sometimes less. Quality over quantity.
